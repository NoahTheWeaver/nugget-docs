# How Per Diems Work at Nugget

::: tip Required Permissions
**Timesheets > User: own timesheets only** or higher. All field service engineers have this by default.
:::

## What is per diem?

Nugget uses a per diem model to simplify how we handle travel expenses. Use your Nugget credit card to pay for hotels, flights, and transportation directly; meanwhile, your per diem is yours to use for anything you want (food, coffee, entertainment, etc.).

Per diem is a flat daily cash payment based on how many hours you work away from your home or office on Nugget-related work. You don't need to submit receipts for it — it's paid out with your next paycheck, in addition to your regular compensation.

| Hours away from home/office in a day | Per diem payment |
|--------------------------------------|-----------------|
| 8 or more hours | $100 (full day) |
| 4 to 8 hours | $50 (half day) |
| Less than 4 hours | No per diem |

Hours are totaled across all eligible jobs for a given day. If you work two jobs in one day, both count toward your total.

Per diem is calculated on a **door-to-door** basis. Your clock starts when you leave home and stops when you get back. Travel to and from the job site counts. Downtime at a hotel does not — only time actively traveling or working.

## What you need to do

**Just log your time accurately.** That's it. Per diem is calculated automatically from your time entries — no separate forms, no trip logging, no expense reports.

1. Open your assigned job in Field Service
2. Go to the **Time Entries** tab
3. Click **Log time** and enter your hours for the day
4. Include all time away from home or office: travel, on-site work, and wrap-up

As soon as your time is saved, a per diem record is created automatically. Noah or accounting will review and process it with your next paycheck.

## Which jobs qualify?

Your dispatcher or admin marks jobs as **Per Diem Eligible** when they create them. If a job has this toggle on, all time you log against it counts toward your per diem.

If you think a job should be eligible but isn't, ask your dispatcher — they can toggle it on the task.

## Scenarios

**Scenario 1:**
You fly out on a business trip at 8 AM and return the same day at 8 PM. Because you were away for 12 hours, you earn a full $100 per diem.

**Scenario 2:**
You leave at 2 PM on Monday and return at 7 AM Tuesday. You were away 10 hours on Monday, so you earn $100 for Monday. No per diem is earned for Tuesday since you only traveled 7 hours.

**Scenario 3:**
You have a two-day trip, leaving at 9 AM on Day 1 and returning at 5 PM on Day 2. You earn $100 for Day 1 and $100 for Day 2, totaling $200.

**Scenario 4 (half day):**
You drive to a client site in the Houston area, leaving at 8 AM and returning home at 1 PM. That's 5 hours door-to-door. You earn a $50 half-day per diem.
