# Field Service

Guides for field service engineers and dispatch coordinators. Scheduling visits, running the service call, closing it out, and making sure your time and parts land in the right place.

::: tip New to Wyatt?
Before you dig in, read [Core Concepts](/core-concepts). It covers the building blocks every app in Wyatt shares.
:::

## Apps You'll Use in Field Service

Out of the full [Apps Overview](/user-guides/apps), these are the ones an FSE or dispatcher opens during a normal week. Everything else you can skip unless a teammate sends you there.

| App | Why you'll open it |
|:-:|---|
| <img src="/screenshots/apps/09_project.png" alt="Project" width="140" /> | Your task list and schedule. Each service visit is a task here. Use the Gantt view to see your week. |
| <img src="/screenshots/apps/21_service.png" alt="Service" width="140" /> | The service request attached to each visit. Where you record what system you worked on, findings, resolution code, and the report. |
| <img src="/screenshots/apps/15_timesheets.png" alt="Timesheets" width="140" /> | Log hours against the task. Drives per diem and labor cost. |
| <img src="/screenshots/apps/06_inventory.png" alt="Inventory" width="140" /> | Pull parts from your truck stock or the warehouse. Log what you used on the service request. |
| <img src="/screenshots/apps/04_contacts.png" alt="Contacts" width="140" /> | The customer record. The *Systems* tab lists the instruments they own and whether each is under contract. |
| <img src="/screenshots/apps/08_helpdesk.png" alt="Helpdesk" width="140" /> | Customer-opened tickets that may turn into service visits. Dispatch triages; FSEs review details. |
